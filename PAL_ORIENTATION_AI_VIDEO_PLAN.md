# PAL Orientation AI Video Plan

Source: `Orientation Form English (Revised).pdf`

## Recommended Format

Create a professional 10-14 minute PAL Environmental orientation video with a clear narrator, jobsite visuals, simple on-screen bullets, and short section breaks. The video should feel like an official safety orientation: direct, serious, easy to follow, and built for workers watching on a phone, iPad, or laptop.

Recommended structure:

- Intro and expectations
- Required training and PPE
- Core jobsite hazards
- PAL-specific environmental and emergency rules
- Reporting, discipline, drug/alcohol, and questions
- Final acknowledgement screen

## Video Tone

The video should not feel like a commercial. It should feel like PAL is speaking directly to new employees before they enter a jobsite.

Voice style:

- Calm
- Confident
- Professional
- Field-friendly
- Clear enough for workers watching on phones

Visual style:

- PAL logo / company header
- Construction and environmental jobsite imagery
- Workers wearing PPE
- Simple icons for PPE, fall protection, fire, emergency, reporting, and drug-free workplace
- No cluttered slides
- No tiny text

## Scene-By-Scene Script

### Scene 1 - Opening

Approx. 30 seconds

Narration:

Welcome to PAL Environmental Services orientation. This training introduces the safety expectations, required training records, jobsite rules, and reporting procedures every PAL employee must understand before working on a project. Your safety, the safety of your crew, and the protection of the environment are PAL priorities on every job.

On-screen text:

- PAL Environmental Services
- Employee Orientation
- Safety. Compliance. Communication.

AI video prompt:

Professional construction safety orientation opening. PAL Environmental Services style. Clean office-to-jobsite transition. Workers entering a controlled construction site wearing hard hats, high visibility vests, safety glasses, gloves, and boots. Corporate safety training look.

### Scene 2 - Orientation Goals

Approx. 40 seconds

Narration:

PAL orientation is built around five goals: protecting employee health and safety, preventing accidents through planning and risk assessment, educating employees, meeting or exceeding safety requirements, and keeping clear communication between employees, supervisors, and management.

On-screen text:

- Protection
- Prevention
- Education
- Compliance
- Communication

AI video prompt:

Modern safety training graphic showing five orientation goals around a jobsite supervisor speaking with a crew. Clean professional layout, blue and green PAL-inspired color palette, realistic construction background.

### Scene 3 - Required Training Records

Approx. 45 seconds

Narration:

Before working on a PAL project, employees must have the required training records for the work they will perform. At minimum, this may include NYC Site Safety Training or OSHA 30, fall prevention training, scaffold user training, and fit test and medical clearance when respiratory protection is required.

On-screen text:

- SST or OSHA 30
- Fall Prevention
- Scaffold User
- Fit Test / Medical Clearance when required

AI video prompt:

Safety coordinator reviewing training cards and certification documents with a construction worker at a jobsite trailer. Clear tablet checklist, professional safety onboarding atmosphere.

### Scene 4 - PPE Required At All Times

Approx. 60 seconds

Narration:

PAL employees must report to the job prepared with required personal protective equipment. At a minimum, workers must wear an approved hard hat, high visibility vest, approved safety glasses, work boots, long pants, and proper shirt coverage. Some tasks may require additional PPE such as face shields, respirators, coveralls, hearing protection, rubber gloves, rubber boots, or task-specific gloves. Respirators may only be used after the proper medical clearance, fit test, and authorization.

On-screen text:

- Hard hat
- Hi-vis vest
- Safety glasses
- Work boots
- Gloves
- Task-specific PPE as required

AI video prompt:

Realistic construction worker PPE check before entering jobsite. Close-up shots of hard hat, safety glasses, high visibility vest, gloves, work boots, and respirator stored properly. Professional safety video.

### Scene 5 - Fall Protection

Approx. 70 seconds

Narration:

Any employee exposed to a fall hazard of six feet or greater must have fall protection training. Fall hazards include open edges, aerial lifts, scaffolds over six feet, and walkways bridging gaps. Guardrails, hole covers, and personal fall arrest systems must be properly installed, inspected, and used. Always inspect fall protection equipment before use, confirm anchor points are rated correctly, watch for overhead hazards, and barricade work below when needed.

On-screen text:

- Six feet or greater
- Inspect before use
- Guardrails and hole covers
- Rated anchor points
- Barricade below

AI video prompt:

Construction safety scene showing guarded edge, secured hole cover marked cover, worker inspecting harness and lanyard, aerial lift with worker tied off. Realistic professional training imagery.

### Scene 6 - Electrical Safety

Approx. 55 seconds

Narration:

Electrical safety starts with inspecting cords, plugs, and temporary wiring before use. Extension cords must be heavy-duty, grounded, undamaged, and used with GFCI protection. Never alter plug prongs, never use cords with exposed wires, and do not route cords through doorways or across walking paths where they create trip hazards. GFCIs must be tested before use each shift.

On-screen text:

- Inspect cords
- Use GFCI
- No exposed wires
- Do not alter plugs
- Keep cords off walkways

AI video prompt:

Safety close-up of a worker inspecting an extension cord and testing a GFCI on a construction site. Damaged cord tagged out of service. Clean safety training style.

### Scene 7 - Ladder Safety

Approx. 70 seconds

Narration:

Ladders should only be used for access or short-duration light-duty tasks when safer options are not practical. A-frame ladders must be fully opened with spreaders locked. Do not stand on the top step or straddle the top. Face the ladder while working and maintain three points of contact when climbing. Straight ladders must be set at the proper slope, tied off, and extend three feet above the landing. Never move a ladder while someone is on it, and never use metal ladders near electrical hazards.

On-screen text:

- Use safer options when possible
- Three points of contact
- No top step
- Tie off straight ladders
- Stay clear of electrical hazards

AI video prompt:

Construction ladder safety demonstration. One correct A-frame setup, one straight ladder tied off extending above landing, worker maintaining three points of contact. Professional training video.

### Scene 8 - Tools, LOTO, and Material Handling

Approx. 90 seconds

Narration:

Employees must be trained before using hand or power tools. Inspect tools, cords, guards, and accessories before each use. Damaged tools must be removed from service and reported. Use the correct PPE, keep solid footing, and unplug tools before changing accessories.

When equipment or systems contain hazardous energy, lockout/tagout procedures are required. Energy sources may include electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, steam, spring, or stored energy. Only trained and authorized personnel may perform lockout/tagout, and only the person who applied a lock may remove it.

Material handling also requires planning. Know the weight and shape of the load, use mechanical assistance when practical, inspect rigging, keep the load close, avoid twisting, secure loads from shifting, and stop to ask for help when something is too heavy or awkward to move safely.

On-screen text:

- Inspect tools
- Remove damaged tools
- Control hazardous energy
- Plan the lift
- Ask for help

AI video prompt:

Split safety training sequence: worker inspecting power tool guard, lockout tag on electrical panel, crew using pallet jack and proper lifting technique. Clean jobsite visuals.

### Scene 9 - Fire Safety and Housekeeping

Approx. 85 seconds

Narration:

Fire prevention means controlling heat, oxygen, and fuel. Know the site-specific emergency action plan. Hot work requires the proper permit and must follow federal, city, local, and project requirements. If a fire is too large, call 911 first. Do not risk your life for equipment.

Housekeeping must be performed daily. Keep walkways, aisles, doorways, stairs, and fire extinguishers clear. Clean spills immediately or barricade the area. Store materials properly and replace poor lighting when needed. A clean jobsite prevents slips, trips, falls, and falling-object hazards.

On-screen text:

- Know the EAP
- Hot work permit required
- Call 911 for serious fires
- Keep access clear
- Clean daily

AI video prompt:

Jobsite fire safety and housekeeping scene. Fire extinguisher visible and unobstructed, clear walkway, organized materials, worker cleaning spill and placing barricade. Professional safety training.

### Scene 10 - HazCom, Scaffolds, and Lifts

Approx. 85 seconds

Narration:

All employees must understand hazard communication, GHS labels, and the location of Safety Data Sheets. Containers must be labeled properly, and SDS information must be available in the event of an exposure.

Scaffolds must be erected by a competent person, installed according to manufacturer requirements, inspected daily, and documented. Never stand or rest on guardrails.

Mobile elevating work platforms and scissor lifts may only be used by trained employees. Workers must wear fall protection when required, stay inside the platform, inspect equipment daily, and maintain awareness above, below, and around the lift.

On-screen text:

- Know SDS location
- Label containers
- Scaffold checklist
- Trained lift operators only
- Inspect daily

AI video prompt:

Safety training montage: SDS binder/right-to-know station, labeled chemical containers, scaffold inspection checklist, scissor lift pre-use inspection with trained worker in harness.

### Scene 11 - Heavy Equipment and Confined Space

Approx. 65 seconds

Narration:

When working near heavy equipment, stay alert. Listen for backup alarms, make eye contact with operators, stay clear of loading areas, and never walk beside equipment being loaded. Operators must use three points of contact when climbing equipment and must watch for overhead power lines and underground utilities.

Confined space entry requires specialized training. Hazards may include low oxygen, hazardous atmosphere, explosive gases, entrapment, or engulfment. Do not enter a confined space unless you are trained and authorized for that specific work.

On-screen text:

- Make eye contact
- Stay out of loading areas
- Watch overhead and underground hazards
- Confined space requires special training

AI video prompt:

Realistic jobsite scene with excavator and workers staying outside swing radius, eye contact with operator, then confined space warning sign with worker stopped before entry. Professional safety video.

### Scene 12 - Asbestos, Lead, Silica, and Environmental Issues

Approx. 95 seconds

Narration:

Only authorized and trained personnel may handle or remove asbestos-containing materials or lead. PAL and all federal, state, local, and project requirements must be followed. Engineering controls, proper PPE, documentation, and hygiene practices are required.

Respirable crystalline silica can cause serious illness. Exposure may occur during cutting, grinding, drilling, chipping, demolition, and other construction tasks. Wet methods, HEPA vacuums, dust collection, medical surveillance when required, and task-specific exposure control plans must be used.

Environmental rules matter on every project. Waste must be disposed of properly. Universal waste, hazardous waste, used oil, batteries, fluorescent bulbs, aerosol cans, and similar materials must not go into regular dumpsters. Contact your supervisor for proper disposal.

On-screen text:

- Authorized workers only
- Use engineering controls
- Control silica dust
- Dispose of waste properly
- Ask supervisor before disposal

AI video prompt:

Environmental construction safety scene showing controlled work area, worker using HEPA vacuum and wet method dust control, labeled waste containers, spill kit, supervisor directing proper disposal.

### Scene 13 - Emergency Action Plan

Approx. 50 seconds

Narration:

Every project must have an emergency action plan posted. Know your muster point before work begins. During an emergency, walk, do not run. Shut down heavy equipment if safe to do so, report to the muster point, and do not leave until released. After notifying proper authorities, PAL supervision and EH&S must be notified. Do not speak to the press. PAL will issue official statements when appropriate.

On-screen text:

- Know the muster point
- Walk, do not run
- Stay until released
- Notify PAL supervision / EH&S
- Do not speak to press

AI video prompt:

Construction site emergency drill. Workers calmly walking to muster point sign, supervisor taking headcount with clipboard, equipment shut down safely. Professional safety training.

### Scene 14 - Discipline, Reporting, and Return to Work

Approx. 90 seconds

Narration:

PAL has disciplinary rules for serious safety violations and repeated non-serious violations. IDLH means immediately dangerous to life or health. These violations can lead to suspension, retraining, or termination depending on the situation and repeat history.

At the same time, no one has the right to put you in an unsafe situation, and no employee will be penalized for refusing unsafe work.

All accidents and incidents must be reported immediately to your supervisor and PAL EH&S, even if they seem minor. Investigations are conducted to find facts, not to assign blame. If medical attention is required, return-to-work notes or discharge papers must be submitted before returning to work. Light duty may be available when authorized by a licensed physician.

On-screen text:

- Stop unsafe work
- Report all incidents immediately
- Fact finding, not fault finding
- Medical paperwork required
- Light duty if authorized

AI video prompt:

Supervisor speaking with worker after a minor incident, first aid/urgent care paperwork, calm professional investigation scene, worker using stop-work authority. Corporate safety training style.

### Scene 15 - Alcohol, Drugs, Smoking, and Testing

Approx. 65 seconds

Narration:

PAL maintains a drug-free and alcohol-free workplace. Alcohol consumption is not allowed, including during lunch breaks. Illegal drugs, controlled substances, drug paraphernalia, or reporting to work under the influence are prohibited. Smoking is not permitted on New York City construction sites. PAL employees and applicants must follow the drug and alcohol testing policy, which may include random testing, post-accident testing, reasonable suspicion testing, return-to-duty testing, follow-up testing, and pre-assignment testing.

On-screen text:

- Zero tolerance
- Drug-free workplace
- No smoking on NYC construction sites
- Testing may be required

AI video prompt:

Serious workplace policy scene. No smoking sign, drug-free workplace notice, worker signing consent form, clean professional safety compliance visual.

### Scene 16 - Closing and Questions

Approx. 40 seconds

Narration:

If you have questions, ask your supervisor, foreman, general superintendent, or PAL EH&S. Safety depends on communication. If you are unsure, stop and ask before continuing. Thank you for completing PAL Environmental Services orientation.

On-screen text:

- Ask questions
- Stop and ask when unsure
- Contact PAL EH&S
- Orientation complete

AI video prompt:

Closing scene for construction safety orientation. Foreman answering worker questions at jobsite meeting, crew wearing PPE, PAL-style closing screen with orientation complete.

## Short Version Option

If the office wants a shorter video, make a 5-7 minute version with these modules:

1. PAL orientation goals and required training
2. PPE and fall protection
3. Electrical, ladders, tools, and LOTO
4. Fire, housekeeping, HazCom, scaffolds, and lifts
5. Environmental hazards: asbestos, lead, silica, and waste
6. Emergency action plan, incident reporting, and drug-free workplace

## AI Voiceover Prompt

Use a clear male or female professional safety instructor voice. The tone should be calm, direct, and easy to understand. Pace should be moderate, with short pauses after important rules. Avoid dramatic music or exaggerated emotion. The voice should sound like an experienced safety trainer speaking to field employees before the start of work.

## AI Video Generation Prompt

Create a professional construction safety orientation video for PAL Environmental Services. The video should use realistic construction and environmental jobsite scenes, workers wearing proper PPE, supervisors reviewing safety rules, clean training graphics, and simple on-screen bullet points. Keep the look serious, official, and field-friendly. Avoid cartoon style. Avoid cluttered slides. Use a corporate safety training tone with blue, white, and green accents.

## Captions Style

Use short caption blocks, not full paragraphs.

Examples:

- Wear required PPE at all times.
- Inspect before use.
- Report incidents immediately.
- Know your muster point.
- Stop and ask when unsure.

## Approval Notes For Office

Before producing the final video, office/safety should confirm:

- Desired video length: 5-7 minutes or 10-14 minutes.
- Whether PAL logo and company colors should be included.
- Whether the EH&S phone number should appear on-screen.
- Whether the drug and alcohol policy should be summarized or shown in more detail.
- Whether the video needs Spanish subtitles or a Spanish version.
- Whether the final video should include a quiz or acknowledgement at the end.
